Question 5 (23 marks)

This question is based on the material you have studied in Unit 2. In particular, it tests your understanding of the following concepts: specification of computational problems, complexity and Python programming.

For parts (b) and (d) of this question, save your Python code in a file called M269_14J_TMA01_Q5_OUCU.py, where ‘OUCU’ is your OU computer username, e.g. abc123. Include this file in your TMA zip file. Also, copy the code into your Solution Document.

On your first day at work as a trainee programmer at GCHQ, on a team dedicated to the decryption of encrypted personal messages sent over the internet, you are given the following short briefing:

A prime number, often just referred to as a prime, is a positive natural number (i.e. a whole number greater than 0) that has no positive divisors other than 1 and itself. For example, 7 is prime because it can only be divided by 1 and 7 leaving a remainder of 0. The number 8 is non-prime because it is divisible by both 2 and 4 with remainder 0.

Prime numbers play a key role in modern cryptographic algorithms, so a thorough understanding of primes and primality is vital in our work. Therefore, we first of all require a program that will generate all the prime numbers that are less than or equal to n, where n is specified as an input.

After seeking help from colleagues, you are given the following extra information:

  1. 1 is not a prime so there is no need to consider it.
  2. An efficient way to test a candidate number for primality is to divide it by every prime that has been discovered up to this point. If any of these divisions leaves remainder 0 then the number is not prime.

You come up with the following initial insight.

Generate primes – initial insight

Create an empty list P to hold the primes generated and add 2 (the first prime) to it. For each j from 3 to n, divide j by each of the primes in P. If none of these divisions leaves a remainder of 0 then add j to P. Return P.

You have been given three initial tasks relating to the prime number problem. They are as follows:

  • a.Using the notation you studied in M269 (yes, GCHQ knows all about us), write a complete specification of the computational problem of generating a list of the prime numbers that are less than or equal to n.

    (3 marks)

  • b.Write a Python function called basicPrime that implements your solution to the computational problem as you have specified it.

    (5 marks)

  • c.Estimate the run-time complexity of your program, using Big-O notation. You do not need to calculate the T(n) of the program, but you should justify your answer.

    (3 marks)

    Shortly after you complete these tasks, your supervisor takes you into a breakout room and explains that such problems as prime number generation are done much more efficiently using a type of algorithm known as a sieve. Instead of generating successive numbers and then testing them for primality, a much better approach is to systematically exclude numbers that cannot possibly be prime. Whatever numbers are left must be prime. The analogy is of sieving out larger and larger particles from some mass, until only the largest remain.

    Your supervisor sketched an outline of a simple version of a sieve algorithm, known as The Sieve of Eratosthenes, on a whiteboard. For a calculation of the primes less than or equal to n:

    create an empty list P to hold primes

    create a sieve list S of length n + 1. Set every element in S to True

    ITERATE over p from 2 to n + 1

    IF the element at p in S is True

    add the element at p in S to P

    generate multiples m of p up to n in increments of p (e.g. initially, these will be 2p, 3p, 4p, etc.); do not include p itself

    as these cannot possibly be primes, set the elements at all m in S to False

    return P

  • d.Write a Python function that implements this algorithm.

    (5 marks)

    Your supervisor now points out that, although Eratosthenes’ algorithm has been known since ancient times, it is not necessarily the most efficient form of sieve for the purpose of generating the prime numbers, and gives you one further task.

  • e.Spend half an hour researching refinements of sieve algorithms and then produce a report of no more than 400 words describing an alternative known as the Sieve of Atkin. Briefly describe how the sieve works and provide enough detail for a developer to implement the algorithm. You are not required to justify the mathematical reasoning behind the algorithm, or to produce detailed structured English. We recommend that you consult at least two sources and include a reference for each. (References will not count towards the word count.)

    (7 marks)
